Everything below is delivered for free for one metro x one segment. The point: if this is what he gives away, imagine what the paid service looks like.

1. **Scored list with dossiers** — 25+ leads ranked by acquisition readiness. Company summary, signals that fired, why they're outgrowing their current setup, suggested angle. Saved as CSV.

3. **Google Sheet** — Final data pushed to a clean, formatted Google Sheet. Dated title, formatted columns, frozen headers, filters on. View-only link.
4. **Interactive hosted report** — Clickable web dashboard with per-lead dossier pages. Not a CSV dump — a real tool their reps can browse.

7. **Methodology + scoring model** — ICP definition, data sources, signal explanations, scoring formula and weights, known limitations, how the feedback loop tunes the model. Full transparency — the client can challenge and co-tune the weights with their sales team's input.
8. **Refresh cadence document** — How weekly refreshes work. What gets re-scraped, what stays cached, how new leads enter the pipeline. Frames the ongoing relationship.

### The feedback loop (the core differentiator)

This is what separates this from a static list vendor.

- After each week, the client reports back on outcomes: conversations, booked meetings, rejections, no-answers, wrong-fit
- Every outcome — positive or negative — feeds back into the scoring model
- A rejection is just as valuable as a booking: it tells us what signal combination to deprioritize
- 15+ years of B2B SaaS sales experience means we know how to read these patterns and turn them into scoring adjustments
- **The longer we work together, the more the list is tuned to YOUR specific win patterns** — not a generic model

This creates a switching cost without a lock-in contract. The model is calibrated to their market, their reps, their close patterns. Starting over with a new vendor means starting the learning curve over.

### Ongoing service includes
- **Weekly refresh** — new scored list every week, stale leads drop off
- **Feedback loop** — outcomes tune the model to their win patterns
- **Multi-metro / multi-segment expansion** — add new lists anytime
- **Scoring model co-tuning** — their sales team's input adjusts the weights
- **Compounding intelligence** — list gets better every single week

### Why NOT per-lead or per-meeting pricing
- **Per-lead** commoditizes the work — puts us in the same mental bucket as a $0.10 Apollo export
- **Per-meeting** is an attribution nightmare — meetings come from nurture sequences, timing, follow-ups. Too hard to track, too easy to argue about.
- **Per signal list** sells the intelligence layer, not individual records

## Sales call structure (logic, not script)

**Opening:** "You got on this call because you saw what our signal lists look like. What I want to do is learn about which metros you're selling into and what your reps' workflow looks like, so I can tell you whether this data would actually move your pipeline. Then I'll walk you through exactly what's in the weekly deliverable. Then we talk pricing. Sound fair?"

**Middle:** Walk through the 8 free-week deliverables. Show the live Phoenix example. Let them click through the dossiers. This IS the sales call — the product sells itself because it's tangible.

**Close:** If deliverables alone don't close → bring up the risk reversal. "I'll build the first list for your team, custom to your ICP, for free. You'll have it in hand before you spend a dollar. If your team doesn't find it useful, we part ways."

**Key reframe:** You're not selling "better leads" (air). You're selling 8 specific things they receive for free, then 4 recurring deliverables plus a learning engine. The conversation is about what they're buying, not whether it'll work.
