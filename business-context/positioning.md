# Positioning — Why Us

## Competitive landscape

### 1. Apollo / ZoomInfo (primary comparison)
- **What they get right:** Massive database, firmographic filters, contact info, intent signals at the account level. Every sales team already has one.
- **Where they fall short:** They can tell you "here are 300 HVAC contractors in Phoenix." They CANNOT tell you which of those 300 are in pain right now — who's hiring dispatchers, who's getting slammed in reviews for missed callbacks, who has no FSM platform, who's scaling fast. Apollo gives you a list. We give you a ranked, scored call sheet with reasons to call each one.

### 2. Internal BDR research (secondary comparison)
- **What they get right:** A good rep doing manual research can find some of these signals one contractor at a time — checking Google reviews, scanning job boards, looking at websites.
- **Where they fall short:** It takes hours per lead. A BDR who spends half their day researching is a BDR who's not calling. We deliver 25+ scored leads per metro per week — that's the output of a full-time researcher, delivered Monday morning before their reps start dialing.

### 3. Lead gen / appointment setting agencies (tertiary comparison)
- **What they get right:** Done-for-you outbound. The client doesn't have to think about it.
- **Where they fall short:** They use the same Apollo/ZoomInfo lists everyone else uses. Their "secret sauce" is copy and volume, not signal quality. We're not competing with them — we're the layer that makes their (or the client's internal) outbound actually work. If the client wants us to run outbound too, that's an upsell.

## Core differentiator

**"Built by a sales vet, not a data vendor."**

Anyone technical could eventually build this pipeline. The moat isn't the code — it's the judgment behind the scoring model. Shane has 15+ years of direct B2B SaaS sales. He built the tool he wished he had when he was the one making cold calls. He knows what signals actually predict a pick-up and a conversation, not just what looks interesting in a spreadsheet.

The scoring model reflects what an experienced rep would research if they had unlimited time: review complaint patterns, dispatch hiring as a scaling signal, FSM platform absence as greenfield opportunity, review velocity as a growth indicator, license tenure as a maturity signal. It's a senior rep's research instinct, automated and delivered weekly at scale.

**What we can credibly claim:**
- We surface buying signals that don't exist in any sales intelligence platform
- We score and rank leads by acquisition readiness, not just firmographics
- We deliver a new scored list every week — this isn't a one-time data pull
- We can spin up any US metro in days
- The free week proves it before they spend a dollar

## Anti-ICP — Who we're NOT for

### Enterprise FSM (ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber tier)
- 500+ employees, internal data teams, enterprise procurement cycles
- They'd want SOC 2 compliance, SLAs, dedicated CSMs — overhead that doesn't match a freelancer model
- **Graceful disqualification:** Don't pursue. They're not in the target company list.

### Companies that only want appointment setting (no list purchase)
- If they want a done-for-you outbound agency and nothing else, we're not the best fit for the core offer
- **However:** This is an upsell opportunity, not a hard no. Lead with the list. If they want us to also run sequences and make calls, scope that as a separate engagement on top.

### Companies with no outbound motion and no plan to start one
- If they're 100% inbound/partner-driven and have no reps making outbound calls, the list has no one to use it
- These prospects self-select out — they won't respond to outbound about outbound

### Red flags in the sales process
- They want to negotiate the free week (if free isn't enough to get started, nothing will be)
- They ask for exclusivity on a metro before paying (wanting to lock up data without commitment)
- They can't articulate which metros they sell into (means they don't have a real outbound motion)
- They want to "see the methodology" before seeing the output (over-indexing on process vs. result)
