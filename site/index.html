The dossiers

Intelligence dossiers built for salespeople. Not for marketers. Not for GTM engineers.

Most lead intel is designed to impress the person who bought the tool — not the rep who has to use it. Our dossiers contain exactly what a salesperson would want open on their screen before they dial: real context, real talking points, real reasons to call today.

01

"Here's what to say on the call"

Not a signal score. Not a keyword. An actual opening line tied to something real that happened at this company this week. Read it, dial, connect.

02

What their customers are saying

Verbatim quotes from real reviews and public feedback. Your rep drops one on the call — "I noticed your customers are raving about X but frustrated by Y" — and the prospect leans in.

03

Who they're hiring and why it matters

Not just "3 open roles." We tell your rep what those hires mean — they're scaling ops, they're replacing a vendor, they're building a team they don't have yet. That's a conversation starter.

04

What tech they run (and what's missing)

Apollo tells you their industry. We tell your rep which tools are on their site, which ones aren't, and what that gap means for your product. That's a different call.

05

Which competitor they just left

Public evidence of switching behavior — vendor changes, negative mentions, migration signals. Your rep says "I saw you moved off [Competitor]" and instantly has credibility.

06

Are they growing or stalling?

Not a "growth score." Actual evidence — review surges, new locations, hiring sprees, online activity spikes. Your rep knows whether to pitch scale or survival.

07

Names, roles, and who to ask for

Decision makers verified, plus the names of people on their team pulled from public sources. Your rep asks for someone by name and skips the gatekeeper.

08

How they actually operate

Response patterns, service quality signals, capacity indicators — pulled from real data, not a database field. Your rep understands their day-to-day before saying hello.

09

How established they are

Years in business, certifications, licensing, public footprint. Your rep knows if they're talking to a founder who started last year or an owner running a 20-year operation — and adjusts the pitch.

None of this exists in Apollo. None of it exists in ZoomInfo or Clay. Your rep picks up the phone and says "I've been researching your company" — and actually means it.
